Crime and Punishment (1867)

Crime and Punishment by Fyodor Dostoevsky is a psychological novel that follows Rodion Raskolnikov, a poor student who commits a murder and struggles with guilt. The story explores his inner conflict as he tries to justify his actions while being consumed by anxiety and remorse. Through his journey, the novel examines themes of morality, justice, and redemption.

I recommend this book because it offers deep insight into human psychology and moral dilemmas. It also challenges readers to reflect on the consequences of their actions and the meaning of true justice.
